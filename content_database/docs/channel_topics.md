## Economy

### Inequality & Wealth Distribution
- How economic crises transfer wealth from poor to rich
- Rich accumulating savings during COVID while workers lose income
- Circular flow of money: spending by the rich drives wages, which flow back to the rich via rent and bills
- Asset ownership as the dividing line between rich and poor
- GDP growth vs inequality divergence
- "Greatest wealth heist" framing of post-crisis policy responses
- Wealth vs income: fundamental distinction (wealth from ownership, income from work)
- Intergenerational wealth trap: born poor, die poor; born rich, die richer
- Passive income generated from wealth ownership (e.g. £2m generating £60k/year)
- High inequality preventing economic recovery
- Billionaire wealth growth during pandemic (171 new billionaires, top 3 UK families gained £20bn)
- 1% vs everyone else as the real divide
- Middle class disappearing: property-owning middle class being eaten by the rich
- Compound wealth: exponential growth advantage for existing wealth holders
- "Divide and rule" strategy by the super-rich to prevent collective action
- The asset economy: shift from work-based income to asset-based wealth determination
- Rent, profit, and interest as the same extraction mechanism
- Geographic inequality: high streets dying, mega-cities vs dead smaller towns

### Housing
- Housing affordability crisis for young people
- House prices doubling after quantitative easing (post-2008)
- Homeownership rates falling (65% to 27% among young people)
- Being locked out of the housing market permanently
- Mortgage debt as "negative wealth" for ordinary people
- Stamp duty holiday and its effects
- 95% mortgage guarantees (2021 Budget)
- Affordability crisis driven by inequality, not supply
- Housing expensiveness as a global phenomenon, not a local planning problem
- London average house price (£600k) vs average wage (£30k)
- Parental wealth gifts as hidden requirement for homeownership
- Rich buying houses that ordinary families need
- Interest rate effects on house prices and mortgages
- Equity release schemes and elder care funding eroding family wealth
- Housing as wealth storage, not shelter
- Your house price going up is bad for your kids

### Wages, Employment & Living Standards
- Unemployment rising during crises
- Wages stagnating over 15+ years while prices rise
- Living standards declining for ordinary people
- Workers on furlough receiving less than pre-crisis income
- Lack of consumer demand and purchasing power
- National living wage rises as too little too late
- Rising costs (food, fuel, petrol) eroding wage gains
- Universal credit reductions affecting low-income people
- Job vacancies not translating to higher wages
- Workers "on sale": rich accumulated purchasing power makes wages cheap
- Currency devaluation making working people poorer in real terms
- Wages collapsing against real assets (property, food, energy)
- Victorian levels of poverty returning to the UK

### Economic Theory & Models
- Demand-side vs supply-side economics
- Trickle-down economics critique (14 years of zero rates = lowest investment in British history)
- Financialisation vs productive economy
- Structural inequality as an economic drag
- Role of economists in influencing policy
- Keynes' 15-hour work week prediction and why it failed
- Why economists are always wrong: systematic failure to predict post-2008 stagnation
- Economics academia disconnected from real-world problems
- Lack of social diversity in economics (least diverse PhD field)
- Key thinkers: Piketty, Zucman, Mian & Sufi, Ha-Joon Chang
- What is money: creation always involves simultaneous debt creation; total money + debt = zero
- GDP meaningless when concentrated among the wealthy
- Capitalism without equal wealth distribution concentrates power
- Money as a token representing value, not a finite physical resource
- Game theory misinterpretation: Prisoner's Dilemma wrongly assumed selfish behaviour
- Growth as insufficient solution to inequality ("why growth is stupid")
- Asset price inflation excluded from CPI measures

### Financial Crises
- 2008 financial crisis: parallels with COVID crisis
- How crises benefit asset owners
- Banking sector collapse and government bailouts
- 12+ years of stagnation after 2008
- Liz Truss mini-budget disaster and market reaction (2022)
- Recession definition irrelevant to actual suffering of ordinary people

## Finance & Markets

### Monetary Policy & Central Banking
- Quantitative easing / money printing by the Bank of England (£450bn during COVID)
- How newly created money flows through the economy
- Government borrowing from the central bank
- Interest rates at zero for 12+ years (since 2008)
- Helicopter money and unconventional policy
- Asset price inflation as a consequence of QE
- Central bank independence vs government institution status (independence established 1998)
- Distinction between street inflation and asset price inflation
- Rising interest rates: benefits lenders (wealthy), harms borrowers (mortgagees, government)
- Interest rate rises as flawed response to inequality-driven inflation
- QE vs traditional borrowing distinction (£450bn QE + £250bn borrowing = £700bn total)
- £700bn COVID spending: where did it go?
- Money devaluation through monetary expansion
- Printing more money doesn't increase real wealth

### Asset Prices & Financial Markets
- Stock market booms despite economic dysfunction
- Tech stocks (Nasdaq) surging
- Bond markets (e.g. 30-year UK bonds)
- Gold as a hedge for the wealthy
- Housing and real estate as asset classes

- Government debt rising above 100% of GDP
- Austerity as a political response to government debt (post-2008)
- Furlough scheme and income support during COVID
- Government stimulus spending vs monetary policy
- Whether government debt needs to be "paid back"
- Budget policies that disproportionately benefit the wealthy (2021 Budget)

- Social care funding mechanisms and who pays

- Spring Statement 2022 and mini-budget 2022 analysis
- "Levelling up" as empty rhetoric (£700bn to rich vs £2.8bn levelling up)

### Tax Policy & Reform
- Wealth tax vs income tax
- Taxing asset ownership rather than work
- Making the rich contribute to crisis recovery
- Historical tax rates (1970s 90% top rate)
- Non-work income (capital gains, trusts) taxed less effectively than employment income
- Wealth Tax Commission recommendations (1% on millionaires)
- Billionaires paying lower effective tax rates than ordinary workers
- Rishi Sunak's personal wealth and responsibility
- Wealth tax mechanics and thresholds (e.g. £2.5m+)
- Post-WWII comparison: higher taxes on rich correlated with affordable housing
- "Wealth Time Limit" proposal: 120-year inheritance window forces wealth circulation
- Who actually pays taxes: misleading statistics about top 1% paying 28% of income tax
- Tax system is regressive for ultra-wealthy (asset income at 10% vs work income at 60%)
- How the rich make taxes unpopular
- Capital flight myth: billionaire assets are immobile, they won't leave
- Historical evidence of successful wealth taxation (post-WWII, etc.)
- Wealth tax implementation feasibility and design
- Universal basic income financing requires wealth taxation

### Tax Avoidance & Loopholes
- Stamp duty exemption for buying 7+ properties at once (Jeremy Hunt example)
- Capital gains and trusts as tax avoidance mechanisms
- Moving money between countries to avoid tax
- Wealthy exploiting loopholes via tax accountants
- Pandora Papers and why politicians don't close loopholes
- Politicians' post-government careers at corporations (Blair, Cameron, Osborne, Clegg)
- Conflict of interest: politicians who become ultra-wealthy won't tax the rich
- Private wealth lawyers helping rich avoid tax (Stephanie Brobbey interviews)
- Duke of Westminster inheriting £9bn paying zero tax
- IEA (Institute of Economic Affairs) promoting policies that benefit the wealthy
- Russian oligarch sanctions show governments CAN seize/tax assets when they choose
